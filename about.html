About the Goldbach Orbit Method

This site demonstrates a new approach to verifying the famous Goldbach Conjecture, which states:

Every even number greater than 2 can be written as the sum of two prime numbers.

Instead of testing all possible pairs (p, q) such that p + q = E, the Goldbach Orbit Method introduces a predictive strategy based on the square root of E:

Key idea: Orbit layers around E/2

We center the search around E/2 and define a theoretical distance:

δ(E) = √E × (log log E) / log E

This tells us where to expect the first valid pair (p, q) to appear.

Layer Classification

  • Central orbit: when p = q = E/2
  • Orbital layer: when |p − E/2| ≤ δ(E)
  • Peripheral region: when the pair lies further away

This classification reveals a hidden structure in how primes align around even numbers. In most cases, the first solution appears inside the central or orbital layer.

Technical Aspects

- The algorithm uses BigInt in JavaScript to support huge numbers.
- The search is split into small batches to avoid freezing your browser.
- It works well even for values like E = 1019 and beyond.
